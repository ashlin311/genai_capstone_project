Aptitude Preparation for Placement Exams

=== Quantitative Aptitude ===

1. Number Series:
Number series questions test your ability to identify patterns.

Types of patterns:
- Arithmetic progression (constant difference): 2, 5, 8, 11, 14, ... (difference = 3)
- Geometric progression (constant ratio): 3, 6, 12, 24, 48, ... (ratio = 2)
- Square series: 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, ... (n^2)
- Cube series: 1, 8, 27, 64, 125, ... (n^3)
- Fibonacci-like: 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, ... (sum of previous two)
- Alternating patterns: 2, 5, 3, 6, 4, 7, ... (two interleaved series)

Practice Question: Find the next number: 2, 6, 12, 20, 30, ?
Answer: 42 (differences are 4, 6, 8, 10, 12 — increasing by 2)

2. Percentages:
Key formulas:
- Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100
- Successive percentage changes: If a value changes by a% and then b%, the net change is (a + b + ab/100)%
- If a price increases by x%, to restore the original price, decrease by (100x)/(100+x)%

Practice Question: A product's price increases by 20% and then decreases by 20%. What is the net change?
Answer: Net change = 20 - 20 - (20×20)/100 = -4%. The price decreases by 4%.

3. Profit and Loss:
- Profit% = (Profit / Cost Price) × 100
- Selling Price = Cost Price + Profit
- If two items are sold at the same price, one at x% profit and the other at x% loss, there is always a net loss of (x²/100)%.

4. Time and Work:
- If A can do a job in 'a' days and B can do it in 'b' days, together they take ab/(a+b) days.
- If A is twice as efficient as B, and B takes 12 days, A takes 6 days.
- Pipe and cistern problems follow the same logic with filling and draining rates.

Practice Question: A can do a piece of work in 10 days and B can do it in 15 days. In how many days can they do it together?
Answer: Together = (10 × 15) / (10 + 15) = 150/25 = 6 days.

5. Time, Speed, and Distance:
- Speed = Distance / Time
- Average speed for two equal distances at speeds a and b = 2ab/(a+b)
- Relative speed: same direction = |a-b|, opposite direction = a+b
- Train problems: add length of train to distance when crossing a platform.

6. Probability:
- P(event) = favorable outcomes / total outcomes
- P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B)
- P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B) if independent
- Complementary: P(not A) = 1 - P(A)

Practice Question: Two dice are thrown. What is the probability that the sum is 7?
Answer: Favorable outcomes = {(1,6),(2,5),(3,4),(4,3),(5,2),(6,1)} = 6. Total = 36. P = 6/36 = 1/6.

7. Permutations and Combinations:
- nPr = n! / (n-r)! (order matters)
- nCr = n! / (r! × (n-r)!) (order doesn't matter)
- Circular permutation = (n-1)!

=== Logical Reasoning ===

1. Coding-Decoding:
- Letter shifting: Each letter shifted by a fixed number (A→D, B→E means shift of +3)
- Reverse order coding: WORD → DROW
- Number coding: A=1, B=2, ..., Z=26

2. Blood Relations:
Key terms to remember:
- Father's or Mother's son = Brother
- Father's or Mother's daughter = Sister
- Father's father = Grandfather
- Mother's brother = Maternal Uncle
- Father's sister = Aunt (Bua)

3. Seating Arrangement:
- Linear arrangement: People in a row facing a direction.
- Circular arrangement: People around a table.
- Draw diagrams to solve these problems.

4. Syllogisms:
Use Venn diagrams to solve.
All A are B. Some B are C.
Conclusion: Some C are A — may or may not be true.

5. Direction Sense:
- Start from a point and track movements.
- Remember: turning right from North = East, turning left from North = West.

=== Verbal Ability ===

1. Reading Comprehension:
- Read the passage carefully before answering.
- Focus on main idea, tone, and inference questions.
- Eliminate obviously wrong answers first.

2. Sentence Correction:
Common errors: subject-verb agreement, tense consistency, pronoun reference, parallelism, dangling modifiers.

3. Vocabulary:
High-frequency words for placement exams:
- Ubiquitous: present everywhere
- Pragmatic: practical, realistic
- Ephemeral: short-lived
- Ambiguous: having multiple meanings
- Meticulous: very careful and precise

=== Aptitude Preparation Strategy ===

1. Practice daily — solve at least 20 problems per day.
2. Focus on weak areas — identify and improve.
3. Time yourself — most exams have strict time limits.
4. Learn shortcuts — for example, Vedic math for multiplication.
5. Take mock tests — simulate real exam conditions.
6. Review mistakes — understand why you got a problem wrong.
7. Focus on high-weightage topics: Number series, percentages, and time & work appear frequently.
