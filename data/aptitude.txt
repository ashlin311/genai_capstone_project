APTITUDE PREPARATION FOR PLACEMENT INTERVIEWS
==============================================

Topic: Quantitative Aptitude, Logical Reasoning, and Verbal Ability for Campus Placements

---

OVERVIEW
--------
Aptitude tests are the first round in most placement processes — TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Accenture, Cognizant, and other companies use them to shortlist candidates before the technical interview. Scoring well in aptitude requires speed, accuracy, and pattern recognition. This document covers all major aptitude topics with key formulas, tricks, and frequently asked question types.

---

SECTION 1: NUMBER SYSTEM
-------------------------
Core Concepts:
- Natural numbers: 1, 2, 3, ...
- Whole numbers: 0, 1, 2, ...
- Integers: ..., -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, ...
- Rational numbers: numbers expressible as p/q
- Prime numbers: divisible only by 1 and itself (2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13 ...)
- Composite numbers: more than two factors
- LCM (Least Common Multiple) and HCF (Highest Common Factor)

Key Formulas:
- LCM × HCF = Product of two numbers
- Sum of first n natural numbers = n(n+1)/2
- Sum of squares of first n natural numbers = n(n+1)(2n+1)/6
- Sum of cubes of first n natural numbers = [n(n+1)/2]²

Frequently Asked Questions:
- Find the LCM of 12, 18, 24 → Answer: 72
- What is the HCF of 48 and 64? → Answer: 16
- How many prime numbers exist between 1 and 50? → Answer: 15
- Find the remainder when 2^10 is divided by 7 → Use cyclicity of remainders

Tricks:
- Divisibility by 3: sum of digits divisible by 3
- Divisibility by 9: sum of digits divisible by 9
- Divisibility by 11: alternate digit sum difference divisible by 11
- For last digit patterns: powers of 2 cycle as 2,4,8,6; powers of 3 cycle as 3,9,7,1

SECTION 2: PERCENTAGES
-----------------------
Core Concepts:
- Percentage means per hundred
- Percentage increase = (Increase / Original) × 100
- Percentage decrease = (Decrease / Original) × 100
- If a value increases by x%, to find the original: Multiply by 100/(100+x)

Key Formulas:
- x% of y = y% of x (useful for mental math)
- Successive percentage change: if value changes by a% then b%, net change = a + b + ab/100

Frequently Asked Questions:
- A salary is increased by 20% and then decreased by 20%. Net change? → Loss of 4%
- 40% of what number is 200? → 500
- If price increases by 25%, by what % must consumption fall to keep expenditure same? → 20%

Tricks:
- To calculate 15% quickly: find 10% then add half of that
- To calculate 12.5%: divide by 8
- Fraction equivalents: 1/8 = 12.5%, 1/6 = 16.67%, 1/3 = 33.33%

SECTION 3: PROFIT AND LOSS
---------------------------
Core Concepts:
- Profit = Selling Price (SP) - Cost Price (CP)
- Loss = Cost Price (CP) - Selling Price (SP)
- Profit % = (Profit / CP) × 100
- Loss % = (Loss / CP) × 100

Key Formulas:
- SP = CP × (100 + Profit%) / 100
- CP = SP × 100 / (100 + Profit%)
- If two items sold at same SP, one at x% profit and one at x% loss → always a net loss of x²/100 %
- Marked Price (MP) and Discount: SP = MP × (100 - Discount%) / 100

Frequently Asked Questions:
- An item bought for Rs 800 sold for Rs 1000. Profit %? → 25%
- A shopkeeper marks up 40% and gives 20% discount. Net profit %? → 12%
- If CP of 10 pens = SP of 8 pens, find profit % → 25%

SECTION 4: TIME, SPEED AND DISTANCE
-------------------------------------
Core Concepts:
- Speed = Distance / Time
- Distance = Speed × Time
- Time = Distance / Speed
- Convert km/h to m/s: multiply by 5/18
- Convert m/s to km/h: multiply by 18/5

Key Formulas:
- Relative speed (same direction): |S1 - S2|
- Relative speed (opposite direction): S1 + S2
- Average speed for equal distances: 2S1×S2 / (S1+S2) — NOT arithmetic mean
- Train crossing a pole: time = Length of train / Speed
- Train crossing a platform: time = (Length of train + Length of platform) / Speed

Frequently Asked Questions:
- A train 200m long crosses a platform 300m long at 90 km/h. Time? → 20 seconds
- Two trains start from A and B, 400km apart. Speed 60 and 40 km/h. When do they meet? → After 4 hours
- A man rows 10 km upstream in 2 hours and downstream in 1 hour. Speed of current? → 2.5 km/h

Boats and Streams:
- Speed downstream = Speed of boat + Speed of stream
- Speed upstream = Speed of boat - Speed of stream
- Speed of boat = (Downstream + Upstream) / 2
- Speed of stream = (Downstream - Upstream) / 2

SECTION 5: TIME AND WORK
--------------------------
Core Concepts:
- If A completes work in n days, A's 1 day work = 1/n
- Combined work per day = sum of individual daily work rates
- Work done = Rate × Time

Key Formulas:
- If A does work in 'a' days and B in 'b' days, together: ab/(a+b) days
- If A is twice as fast as B, and B takes n days → A takes n/2 days
- Pipes and cisterns: filling pipe is positive, draining pipe is negative

Frequently Asked Questions:
- A does a job in 10 days, B in 15 days. Together? → 6 days
- A pipe fills tank in 6 hours, leak empties in 12 hours. Net fill time? → 12 hours
- A and B together do work in 6 days. A alone takes 10 days. B alone? → 15 days

SECTION 6: PERMUTATION AND COMBINATION
----------------------------------------
Core Concepts:
- Permutation: arrangement (order matters) → nPr = n! / (n-r)!
- Combination: selection (order doesn't matter) → nCr = n! / r!(n-r)!
- Fundamental counting principle: if event A has m ways and B has n ways → m × n total

Key Formulas:
- nC0 = nCn = 1
- nCr = nC(n-r)
- nCr + nC(r-1) = (n+1)Cr
- Circular permutations: (n-1)!
- Arrangements with repetition: n! / (p! × q! × r!) where p,q,r are repeated elements

Frequently Asked Questions:
- In how many ways can 5 people sit in a row? → 5! = 120
- In how many ways can a committee of 3 be chosen from 8 people? → 8C3 = 56
- How many 3-digit numbers can be formed from digits 1-5 without repetition? → 5×4×3 = 60
- In how many ways can the letters of MISSISSIPPI be arranged? → 11!/(4!×4!×2!) = 34650

SECTION 7: PROBABILITY
------------------------
Core Concepts:
- Probability = Favorable outcomes / Total outcomes
- P(A) is between 0 and 1
- P(A) + P(not A) = 1
- Mutually exclusive events: P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B)
- Independent events: P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B)

Key Formulas:
- P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B)
- Conditional probability: P(A|B) = P(A and B) / P(B)

Frequently Asked Questions:
- A bag has 3 red, 4 blue balls. Probability of picking red? → 3/7
- Two dice rolled. Probability sum = 8? → 5/36
- Cards: probability of getting a king or a heart? → 4/52 + 13/52 - 1/52 = 16/52

SECTION 8: NUMBER SERIES
--------------------------
Types of Series:
- Arithmetic progression (AP): common difference is constant → 2, 5, 8, 11 (d=3)
- Geometric progression (GP): common ratio is constant → 2, 6, 18, 54 (r=3)
- Difference series: differences between consecutive terms form a pattern
- Square/cube series: terms are perfect squares or cubes
- Mixed/alternating series: two interlaced series

AP Formulas:
- nth term = a + (n-1)d
- Sum of n terms = n/2 × [2a + (n-1)d]

GP Formulas:
- nth term = a × r^(n-1)
- Sum of n terms = a(r^n - 1) / (r-1)

Frequently Asked Questions:
- Find next term: 2, 6, 12, 20, 30, ? → 42 (differences: 4,6,8,10,12)
- Find missing: 1, 4, 9, 16, ?, 36 → 25 (perfect squares)
- Find next: 3, 6, 12, 24, ? → 48 (GP, r=2)

SECTION 9: LOGICAL REASONING
------------------------------
Topics Covered:
- Blood relations
- Directions and distances
- Coding-decoding
- Syllogisms
- Seating arrangements
- Data sufficiency
- Clocks and calendars

Blood Relations Quick Tips:
- Map family tree carefully before answering
- Common trick: "Son of my father's only son" = your own son

Direction Sense:
- Always face North initially unless stated
- Turns: Left turn = counterclockwise, Right turn = clockwise

Coding-Decoding:
- Letter position coding: A=1, B=2, ..., Z=26
- Reverse alphabet: A=26, Z=1
- Pattern: shift each letter by a fixed number

Clocks:
- Minute hand moves 360° in 60 min → 6° per minute
- Hour hand moves 360° in 12 hours → 0.5° per minute
- Angle between hands at H hours M minutes = |30H - 5.5M|

SECTION 10: PLACEMENT APTITUDE STRATEGY
-----------------------------------------
Time Management:
- Most aptitude tests: 60-90 minutes for 60-90 questions
- Target: 1 minute per question maximum
- Skip and return strategy: don't get stuck on one question

Section-wise Approach:
- Quantitative: focus on speed using shortcuts
- Logical: read carefully, don't overthink
- Verbal: read passages quickly, focus on tone and inference

Common Company Patterns:
- TCS NQT: Numerical Ability, Verbal Ability, Reasoning, Coding (2 questions)
- Infosys: Puzzle solving, Data Interpretation, Sentence Completion
- Wipro NLTH: Aptitude + Essay Writing
- Accenture: Cognitive Assessment + Coding

Preparation Tips:
- Practice at least 20 mock tests before the actual exam
- Review wrong answers thoroughly — identify the pattern of mistakes
- Learn at least 5 shortcut tricks per topic
- Time yourself during practice — accuracy under pressure matters
- Revise fraction-percentage-decimal equivalents until they are automatic

Important Formulas to Memorize:
- Simple Interest: SI = PRT/100
- Compound Interest: CI = P(1 + R/100)^T - P
- Area of circle: πr²
- Volume of cylinder: πr²h
- Pythagoras: a² + b² = c²
